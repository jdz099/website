The Story of Vagabond

Vagabond is one of the best-selling manga of all time, often regarded as a masterpiece to many. At first glance, a story set in samurai-period Japan with a protagonist inspired by the legendary Miyamoto Musashi may not seem like anything out of the ordinary - but the philosophical themes and unique introspective storytelling makes it like none other, resonating deeply with fans across the world.

The Story

What is Vagabond?

Vagabond is a historical-fiction manga that tells the story of Miyamoto Musashi as the main character, following him and his journey to notoriety. Written and illustrated by Takehiko Inoue, it is closely adapted from a 1939 novel called "Musashi" by Eiji Yoshikawa. Vagabond, however, primairly uses introspective storytelling and focuses more on philosophical aspects, with much of it being based off Musashi's real-life philosophy.

Historical Background

Who was Miyamoto Musashi?

Miyamoto Musashi is widely considered to have been one of the most skillful samurai in all of history, referred to as Japan's "sword saint". By the end of his natural life, he had created a unique technique of swordsmanship and wrote various philosophical works that are still studied today.

Philosophy

The Path of Aloneness

A week before Musashi's death, he compiled a list of twenty-one rules that he believed were important to living a meaningful life. This is called the "Dokkōdō," or "The Path of Aloneness" in English. While some of these rules certainly reflect the time period, many can still be effectively applied today. In Vagabond, the main character Musashi can be seen applying these to his life.

1.

Accept everything the way it is.

2.

Do not seek pleasure for its own sake.

3.

Do not, under any circumstances, depend on a partial feeling.

4.

Think lightly of yourself and deeply of the world.

5.

Be detached from desire your whole life long.

6.

Do not regret what you have done.

7.

Never be jealous.

8.

Never let yourself be saddened by a separation.

9.

Resentment and complaint are appropriate neither for oneself nor others.

10.

Do not let yourself be guided by the feeling of lust or love.

11.

In all things have no preferences.

12.

Be indifferent to where you live.

13.

Do not pursue the taste of good food.

14.

Do not hold on to possessions you no longer need.

15.

Do not act following customary beliefs.

16.

Do not collect weapons or practice with weapons beyond what is useful.

17.

Do not fear death.

18.

Do not seek to possess either goods or fiefs for your old age.

19.

Respect Buddha and the gods without counting on their help.

20.

You may abandon your own body but you must preserve your honor.

21.

Never stray from the Way.
